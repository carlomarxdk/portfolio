Latvian born-and-raised but Denmark-based researcher. I am a PhD Candidate (I have recently submitted my PhD Thesis, defence in February 2024) at the Technical University of Denmark (Section for Cognitive Systems), and a member of the Social Complexity Lab and the Copenhagen Center for Social Data Science at the University of Copenhagen.

My research primarily focused on the Representation of Human Behavior, a key component of the Nation-Scale Social Networks project. I am deeply interested in developing tools that provide profound insights into the evolution of individuals' lives and the spread of ideas within communities.

My work predominantly explored the use of Transformer-based models to analyze sequences of socio-economic data, encompassing labor, health, and education. I work under the supervision of Professor Sune Lehmann and Professor Lars Kai Hansen. For a period of time I visited Professor Tina Eliassi-Rad at the Northeastern University.
