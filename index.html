About me

I am a second-year PhD student at the Technical University of Denmark (Section for Cognitive Systems) and a member of Copenhagen Center for Social Data Science at the University of Copenhagen. My areas of interest include Natural Language Processing, Fondumental Models, Deep Learning, Predictive Analysis, Computational Social Sciences and Algorithmic Ethics.

My research is a part of Nation-Scale Social Networks project, where I focus on the Representation of Human Behavior. I adapt Transformer-based models to analyze and embed sequences of socio-economic data (such as income, health, education) to determine whether we can use these models to predict certain events in a person's life (e.g. death, health conditions, crimes). I work under the supervision of Professor Sune Lehmann and Professor Lars Kai Hansen.

From September 2022 until February 2023, I am visiting Professor Tina Eliassi-Rad at the Network Science Institute (Northeastern University) in Boston, US. Under her supervision, I am exploring the area of the Algorithmic Auditing and Fairness.
